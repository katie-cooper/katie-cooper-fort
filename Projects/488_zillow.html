Fixit Finder

Spring 2025

Fixit Finder is a group project developed for COMP 488 at UNC‚ÄìChapel Hill. The tool assists contractors in the Chapel Hill area by identifying homes likely to require renovation, helping them better target their advertising and outreach.

My Role: Project Leader

As project leader, I managed our team of six, guided decision-making, and ensured that we delivered a functional prototype on time. My responsibilities included:

  • Stakeholder Analysis: Identified contractors as the primary stakeholders and aligned project goals with their needs.
  • Scope Definition: Outlined the features and boundaries of the project to ensure clarity and feasibility.
  • Task Delegation: Assigned responsibilities across data cleaning, model building, and prototype design to balance workload effectively.
  • Timeline Management: Created and maintained project milestones to keep the group on track.
  • Risk Assessment: Anticipated challenges with data quality and tool usability, developing strategies to address them.
  • Deliverables Oversight: Coordinated the production of the final presentation and interactive demo.

Key Features

  • Filtered housing data to identify older homes likely in need of renovation.
  • Developed a recommender system based on home attributes such as year built, bed-to-bath ratio, and remodel indicators.
  • Built a prototype interface to showcase contractor use cases.
