Sepsis Prediction Research

Utilizing the BOLD dataset and machine learning to predict patient deterioration or improvement within 24 hours.

Project Report Reserach Poster" Literature Review"

Role: Researcher

Summer 2024 · University College Dublin · Life Science and Data Analytics Research Group

About the Project

This research focused on leveraging the BOLD dataset to predict whether a patient’s condition would deteriorate or improve over a 24-hour period. The study combined machine learning techniques with feature selection and Explainable AI (XAI) to identify the most critical indicators of patient outcomes.

By applying three different machine learning models and multiple feature selection tools, the research provided new insights into the role of SOFA scores in sepsis prediction. Beyond technical accuracy, the project explored the real-world challenges of applying AI in clinical settings and highlighted areas that require further study.

A key emphasis of this work was bridging the gap between machine learning outputs and clinical understanding. The research considered the design of effective AI user interfaces (UI) for clinicians, focusing on how XAI methods can enhance usability, trust, and decision-making in healthcare environments.

Key Contributions

  • Predicted patient deterioration or improvement using the BOLD dataset
  • Applied three machine learning models with multiple feature selection tools
  • Investigated the role of SOFA scores in patient outcome prediction
  • Explored clinical challenges and future research needs in AI-based sepsis prediction
  • Highlighted the importance of Explainable AI and effective clinician-facing interfaces
